My Favorite Brand  

 3:AM Magazine

As a mass communications student, I enjoy reading many different types of media. One of my favorite ways to consume media is by reading, online or in print. I especially enjoy 3:AM Magazine, which has options from blog posts to poetry pieces. 3:AM is an electronic magazine that has been around since 2000 and allows writers to send in their pieces to the editors who choose what will appear on the website. Like a newspaper, the magazine features different sections including; reviews, essays, interviews, fiction, poetry, and blogs.

 The website design is what makes it stand out though. The banner print is in a typewriter-style print which works well with the 70s-style block art in the background. The header also looks vintage, as if they have kept the same one since when they first began. The rest of the font is a blocky print that looks like it would be printed in a modern newspaper. Along with the banner, there are several options to click on so it is easier to find what you are looking for. There are also buttons to submit stories or individually search one up. Scrolling down reveals featured pieces placed on shadowed rectangles with pictures at the top of them. The featured pieces are arranged by type and have a brief summary as well as the author’s name. This website does a great job of looking aesthetic while also being functional.

 I appreciate how the banner pulls you in, and then everything else is white allowing the featured articles to stand out. I plan to use similar colors and possibly similar fonts. For the font, in the header, I would use a typewriter-style that will stand out with the colors and the overall aesthetic. I found one called Rational TW that I like. I know it's boring, but for the rest of the text, I think Times New Roman would look good. It’s easily recognizable but it's still a good font. For the background color where featured stories are placed, I would use a slightly more off-white color as opposed to the eggshell white that does not necessarily match the banner at the top. The colors I would use for my header would be dusty burgundy (#A43E3C), light navy blue (#204972), and mustard yellow (#204972). I hope to make it look slightly vintage as the colors all together look good when slightly faded.
